Description:
Abstract:
A shoreline analysis was conducted using ESRI's ArcMap Geographic Information Systems software. Ortho-rectified aerial photos conducted by Suffolk County in the spring of 2004 were used to identify shoreline structures. A new tool, Pictometry, which uses oblique images taken in 2006 as well as direct overhead aerial photos (also from 2006), was used to further validate a structures presence as well as what it's intended function was.
Purpose:
To characterize the degree of shoreline hardening in Great South Bay

Groins are structures that are place perpendicular to short originating from the uppermost part of the beach and extend out into the water. The purpose of these structures is to trap sediment as it moves along shore. The result is a loss of sediment downdrift of the groin.

Attribute_Domain_Values:
Enumerated_Domain:
Enumerated_Domain_Value: Piers (Included in Seperate Polygon shapefile- Piers.shp)
Enumerated_Domain_Value_Definition: Piers
Enumerated_Domain_Value_Definition_Source:
Piers were identified in a separate polygon file within this project due to their large size. Piers are structures that are built out into the water. A bulkhead is usually constructed and then fill is placed behind it to create more land. They can be constructed out of bulkheaded material and fill, or wood on top of pilings. Wooden piers are distinct from docks it they significantly impede water and sediment movements.
Attribute_Domain_Values:
Enumerated_Domain:
Enumerated_Domain_Value: RR
Enumerated_Domain_Value_Definition: Rip-Rap
Enumerated_Domain_Value_Definition_Source:
Rip-rap refers to any material laid upon the shoreline to prevent the naturally erosive forces of wind and tide. Rip-rap can consist of piles of broken concrete, torn up asphalt, and quarried boulders. A more organized and effective form of rip-rap known as, gambion, is also included in this feature class. Gambion often consists of rocks and boulders packed into large wire mesh bags. These bags are then laid upon the shore and often bound together to make one larger structure.
